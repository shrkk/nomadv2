**Gathered:** 2026-04-06
**Status:** Ready for planning
**Source:** User description (captured during /gsd-insert-phase)

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

When a user taps a visited (highlighted) country on the globe, a bottom sheet slides up from the panel below showing a rich country + city detail experience. This phase delivers that sheet end-to-end: data fetching, proximity-based city grouping, photo surfacing, temperature display, stats aggregation, and trip log listing.

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Entry Point
- Trigger: user taps a highlighted country on the globe
- Presentation: sheet slides up from the existing bottom panel (not a full-screen push)
- Dismiss: back/chevron control top-left returns to the globe

### Country Header
- Top-left: country name as a prominent header (e.g. "Austria")
- Style: large, bold — matches existing Nomad heading typography

### City Strip (horizontal scroll)
- Immediately below the header
- Horizontally scrollable row of city thumbnail cards
- Cities grouped by geographic proximity (same clustering logic used for trip route grouping)
- Tapping a city card scrolls/snaps the photo carousel to that city

### Photo Carousel (per city)
- Full-width, paged carousel — one city per page
- Shows the user's own photos for that city
- Temperature notch: a pill/capsule centered at the top of the photo card showing temperature
  - Source: WeatherKit current conditions OR historical temperature from trip data
  - Design reference: dark pill with temperature value, similar to the screenshot provided

### Location Identity (below photo)
- Location name: large subheading (e.g. "Obertauern Ski Resort")
- City + country: smaller, lighter subheading below (e.g. "Austria, Salzburg")

### Stats Pill
- Single horizontal pill row below the subheadings
- Three stats separated by dividers: # of logs · km traveled · photos taken
- Data sourced from existing Firestore trip data for that city

### Trip Logs Section
- Below the stats pill
- Lists the user's trip log entries while they were in that city
- Chronological order
- Style: cards or rows consistent with existing trip log display in the app

### Claude's Discretion
- Exact SwiftUI sheet presentation mechanism (detent configuration, drag behavior)
- Temperature fetch strategy (WeatherKit vs stored trip value fallback)
- City proximity clustering algorithm parameters (radius threshold)
- Photo loading/caching approach
- Empty states (no photos, no trips for a city)
- Pagination / lazy loading for cities with many photos

</decisions>
